Hello Sunshine

Studying The disconnect between the younger & older generation

Status: Recruitment Phase

A controlled social experiment testing measurable outcomes of intergenerational connection through structured daily interactions, shared activities, and meals over a four-week period.

Loneliness Reduction Generational Unity Community
Primary Hypothesis

"Structured intergenerational interaction—consisting of daily digital check-ins, shared weekly activities, and regular communal meals—will produce statistically significant reductions in loneliness metrics and generate measurable increases in cross-generational empathy within a four-week timeframe."

Experimental Variables (4-Week Protocol)
V1

Daily Check-ins

Digital morning & evening wellness exchanges via text

V2

Weekend Activities

Shared in-person or virtual experiences (museum, walk, game, workshop)

V3

Weekly Meal

One shared meal every week

Total Experiment Duration: 4 weeks (28 days) | Pre/Post assessments via standardized loneliness & connection scales

Expected Outcomes

Connection Metrics

Measurable increase in weekly social interactions

Myth Disruption

Quantifiable evidence disproving the myth of insurmountable generational disconnect

Loneliness Reduction

Statistically significant decrease in loneliness

Participant Pairing Structure

Younger Group

Group A

  • • Age range: 18-30 years
  • • Daily digital check-ins
  • • Engaging across generational boundaries

Older Group

Group B

  • • Age range: 45+ years
  • • Comfortable with basic digital communication
  • • Interest in sharing stories & wisdom
  • • Curiosity about various perspectives

Total Time Commitment

Approximately 3-5 hours per week across daily check-ins (~1 hr/week), weekend activity (~2 hrs), and weekly meal (~1 hr). Flexible scheduling to accommodate both participants' availability and energy levels.
